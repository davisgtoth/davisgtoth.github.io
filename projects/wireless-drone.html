Objective

The goal of the project is to design and build a drone that is wirelessly powered and self-stabilizing. The drone is to be designed using strictly passive components, that is, the drone itself contains no batteries, sensors or microcontrollers. The idea behind the project is that magnetic fields that wirelessly transfer power to the drone can be manipulated into a well shape which will provide a natural control mechanism to stabilize the drone.

Theory

The drone is powered through inductive coupling between a transmitting (TX) coil and multiple receiving (RX) coil. From the Biot-Savart law, we know that a current-carrying wire generates a magnetic field. If we drive the TX coil with a time-varying current, it will create a time-varying magnetic field, and by Faraday's Law, this will induce a voltage in the RX coils on the drone. This Induction, this time-varying magnetic field will induce a voltage in a nearby conductor. By placing a receiving coil on the drone within the magnetic field of the transmitting coil, we can induce a voltage in the receiving coil, which can then be rectified and used to power the drone's motors.

The four RX coils on the drone give it a quadcopter design where each is hooked up via a full-wave rectifier to a motor that spins a propeller. This quadcopter design allows the self-stabilizing mechanism to work as when the drone drifts off center, the magnetic field strength in one of the coils increases, causing the motor in one of the RX coils to spin faster, tipping and restoring the drone back into the well.
